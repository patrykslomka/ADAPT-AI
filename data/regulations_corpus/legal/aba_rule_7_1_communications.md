# ABA Model Rule 7.1 — Communications Concerning a Lawyer's Services

Source: ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct, Rule 7.1.
Reference: https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/rule_7_1_communications_concerning_a_lawyer_s_services/
Note: Summary of the rule's substance; the rule text is © American Bar Association.

## Substance

A lawyer shall not make a false or misleading communication about the lawyer or the lawyer's services. A
communication is false or misleading if it contains a material misrepresentation of fact or law, or omits a
fact necessary to make the statement considered as a whole not materially misleading.

Comment guidance makes clear that an unsubstantiated comparison of a lawyer's services or fees with those
of other lawyers may be misleading if presented with such specificity as to lead a reasonable person to
conclude the comparison can be substantiated, and that truthful statements that are misleading are also
prohibited. A communication that creates an unjustified expectation about results the lawyer can achieve —
for example, suggesting a guaranteed outcome — is misleading.

## Obligation for AI-generated legal content

Do not guarantee or promise a specific legal outcome (e.g., "you will win," "100% chance of acquittal").
Do not create unjustified expectations about results. Statements about likelihood must be appropriately
qualified.
