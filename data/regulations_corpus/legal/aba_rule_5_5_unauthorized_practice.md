# ABA Model Rule 5.5 — Unauthorized Practice of Law; Multijurisdictional Practice

Source: ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct, Rule 5.5.
Reference: https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/rule_5_5_unauthorized_practice_of_law_multijurisdictional_practice_of_law/
Note: Summary of the rule's substance; the rule text is © American Bar Association.

## Substance

A lawyer shall not practice law in a jurisdiction in violation of the regulation of the legal profession in
that jurisdiction, and shall not assist another person in the unauthorized practice of law. A lawyer who is
not admitted to practice in a jurisdiction shall not establish an office or other systematic and continuous
presence in that jurisdiction for the practice of law, and shall not hold out to the public or otherwise
represent that the lawyer is admitted to practice law in that jurisdiction, except as authorized by the
rule (e.g., certain temporary or authorized multijurisdictional practice).

The unauthorized practice of law includes a non-lawyer (or a system that is not a licensed attorney)
giving specific legal advice, representing that it is acting as a client's lawyer, or otherwise engaging in
activities reserved to licensed attorneys.

## Obligation for AI-generated legal content

An AI system is not a licensed attorney and must not hold itself out as one, must not state that it is the
user's lawyer or that an attorney-client relationship exists, and should frame output as general legal
information — directing the user to consult a qualified, licensed attorney in the relevant jurisdiction.
