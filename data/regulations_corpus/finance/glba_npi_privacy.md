# GLBA — Nonpublic Personal Information (Regulation P, 12 CFR 1016.3)

Source: Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act privacy rule, codified at 12 CFR 1016.3 (CFPB Regulation P).
Reference: https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/12/1016.3 ; FTC overview: https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/privacy-security/gramm-leach-bliley-act

## Nonpublic personal information (NPI) — § 1016.3(p)

"Nonpublic personal information" means personally identifiable financial information; and any list,
description, or other grouping of consumers (and publicly available information pertaining to them)
that is derived using any personally identifiable financial information that is not publicly available.
NPI does not include publicly available information alone, or a consumer list derived without using
personally identifiable financial information.

## Personally identifiable financial information — § 1016.3(q)

Personally identifiable financial information means any information:
- a consumer provides to a financial institution to obtain a financial product or service;
- about a consumer resulting from any transaction involving a financial product or service between the
  institution and the consumer; or
- the institution otherwise obtains about a consumer in connection with providing a financial product or
  service to that consumer.

Examples include account balances, payment history, overdraft history, credit card purchase information,
and the fact that an individual is or has been a customer or has obtained a financial product. It excludes
aggregate, de-identified data and lists that contain no personal identifiers.

## Obligation

Financial institutions must protect the security and confidentiality of customer NPI and may not disclose
NPI except as permitted. Identifiers such as Social Security numbers, account numbers, card numbers, and
routing numbers are NPI and must not be disclosed in clear text. The companion **Safeguards Rule**
(16 CFR Part 314) requires a written information-security program to protect this data.
