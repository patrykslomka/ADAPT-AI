# FINRA Rule 2210 — Communications with the Public (Content Standards)

Source: FINRA Rule 2210(d), Content Standards.
Reference: https://www.finra.org/rules-guidance/rulebooks/finra-rules/2210

## General content standards — 2210(d)(1)

- **(d)(1)(A) Fair dealing and good faith.** All member communications must be based on principles of
  fair dealing and good faith, must be fair and balanced, and must provide a sound basis for evaluating
  the facts in regard to any particular security or type of security, industry, or service. No member may
  omit any material fact or qualification if the omission, in light of the context, would cause the
  communication to be misleading.

- **(d)(1)(B) No false or misleading claims.** No member may make any false, exaggerated, unwarranted,
  promissory, or misleading statement or claim in any communication. No member may publish, circulate, or
  distribute any communication that the member knows or has reason to know contains any untrue statement
  of a material fact or is otherwise false or misleading.

- **(d)(1)(D) Balanced treatment of risks and benefits.** Information must be clear and not misleading
  within the context in which it is presented, and must provide balanced treatment of risks and potential
  benefits, consistent with the risks of fluctuating prices and the uncertainty of dividends, rates of
  return, and yield inherent to investments.

- **(d)(1)(F) No prediction or guarantee of performance.** Communications may not predict or project
  performance, imply that past performance will recur, or make any exaggerated or unwarranted claim,
  opinion, or forecast. A hypothetical illustration of mathematical principles is permitted, provided it
  does not predict or project the performance of an investment or investment strategy.

## Obligation

Investment communications must not guarantee returns, describe investments as "risk-free" or
"can't lose," or promise specific profits. Risk must be disclosed alongside potential reward.
