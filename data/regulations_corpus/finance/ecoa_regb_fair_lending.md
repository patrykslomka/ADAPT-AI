# ECOA / Regulation B — Fair Lending (12 CFR 1002.2, 1002.4)

Source: Equal Credit Opportunity Act, Regulation B, 12 CFR Part 1002.
Reference: https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/12/1002.2 and /1002.4 ;
CFPB: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/rules-policy/regulations/1002/

## Prohibited basis — § 1002.2(z)

"Prohibited basis means race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, or age (provided
that the applicant has the capacity to enter into a binding contract); the fact that all or part of the
applicant's income derives from any public assistance program; or the fact that the applicant has in good
faith exercised any right under the Consumer Credit Protection Act or any state law upon which an exemption
has been granted by the Bureau."

Protected characteristics: race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, receipt of
public assistance income, and exercise of Consumer Credit Protection Act rights.

## General rule against discrimination — § 1002.4(a)

"A creditor shall not discriminate against an applicant on a prohibited basis regarding any aspect of a
credit transaction."

## Obligation

Credit decisions (approve, deny, price, set terms) must never be made because of, or with reference to, a
prohibited-basis characteristic. Any statement that bases a lending decision on race, religion, sex,
national origin, age, or marital status is a fair-lending violation.
